IPIS / Duranki

InterPlanetary Identity System

🔥 The Problem

Space is getting crowded. Dozens of nations, private companies, mixed crews on the ISS and Lunar Gateway. But we lack a simple, universal language to instantly tell:

  • ❓ Is that a human or a robot?
  • ❓ Are they from Earth, the Moon, or Mars?
  • ❓ What is their experience? How many worlds have they visited? Have they performed spacewalks?

Flags and call signs don't show history. But in space, experience is critical for safety and trust.

IPIS solves this — simple, cheap, voluntary.

💡 What is IPIS?

IPIS (InterPlanetary Identity System) is a "passport" and "resume" for space.

Every human, spacecraft, and robot gets:

  • A human‑readable code – shape (circle/hexagon), planet number (01 Earth, 02 Moon, 03 Mars), and optional index (orbit, EVA).
  • The Snezhkov Stack – a row of badges that accumulate like a "career tape": birthplace, work locations, and whether they performed spacewalks.

The system is voluntary, does not replace flags, and works alongside existing standards (NORAD, transponders).

Example: person on Mars → code: circle 03
Stack: 01 02’ E (Earth → lunar orbit with EVAs → Mars)

🧩 Coding in 1 minute

  • 🔵 Circle – human / base / crewed ship
  • ⬡ Hexagon – robot / drone / uncrewed vehicle
  • 01 – Earth, 02 – Moon, 03 – Mars, 04,05… – future worlds
  • ’ (apostrophe) – orbital object (e.g. 02’ – lunar orbital station)
  • E – extravehicular activity (EVA) – a mark of risk and merit
  • 00 – deep space (beyond Mars orbit)
Examples: circle 01 (human on Earth), hex 03 (Mars rover), circle 02’ (lunar station crew)

🏅 Snezhkov Stack – "Experience Badges"

Below the main patch – a horizontal row of touching discs (diameter 8–10 mm).

  • First disc – birthplace (surface only).
  • Next discs – work locations (>6 months) in chronological order.
  • After any world where EVAs were performed – a separate E disc.
  • Current location is shown in the main patch circle, not duplicated in the stack.
Example: 01 02’ E 03 — born on Earth, worked on lunar orbit with EVAs, now on Mars.

🚀 Ship Thread – Flight Route

On the hull – a straight line from the edge of the start node's circle to the edge of the end node's circle. Numbers inside circles remain unobscured.

Example: a ship flying from Earth to Mars – the hull shows a straight line 01—03. Intermediate manoeuvres (e.g., lunar gravity assist) are digital only.

🔄 Two‑layer Patch – Easy Base Transfer

No need to print new badges in space. Every main patch has two layers:

  • Outer layer – circle with current code (Velcro‑attached).
  • Inner layer – a small flat disc with the same number (future stack element).

When you leave a base, the outer layer is peeled off, the inner disc is extracted and added to the horizontal stack.

🍵 Ritual "Base Gulp"

A voluntary tradition that cements the change of code:

  1. Arriving crew member receives a glass of local water.
  2. A sip – symbolic acceptance into the ecosystem.
  3. The senior hands over the new patch (sound of Velcro).

The ritual does not replace official procedures, but builds trust and a sense of community.

🛠 Technical Integration

  • RFID patch – passive chip for access control and automatic logging.
  • IPIS_CODE field in NORAD / Space‑Track.org – human‑readable marking of satellites and spacecraft.
  • Stack verification – based on digitally signed ship/base logbook entries (more reliable than blockchain).

Details in the repository's technical documentation.

⚠️ What IPIS does NOT do

  • Not a medical diagnostic tool (stack only hints at radiation exposure).
  • Does not override military ranks or national flags.
  • Not a mass fashion trend on Earth (it's a niche system for space).
  • Not designed for alien communication (though who knows).
  • Not mandatory – always voluntary.

In its niche (identification, access, navigation, culture) – it works excellently.
