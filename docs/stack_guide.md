# Snezhkov Stack Guide – How to Wear and Update Your IPIS Patch

This guide explains how to physically don, update, and maintain your IPIS patch and the Snezhkov Stack. The system is designed to work without any special equipment – just Velcro, your hands, and the two‑layer patch itself.

---

## 1. The Two‑Layer Patch (Anatomy)

Every **main patch** (the large circle showing your current location) has two layers:

- **Outer layer** – the visible circle with your current code (e.g. `circle 01`). Attached with Velcro to the inner layer.
- **Inner layer** – a flat, small disc (same diameter as a stack disc, 8–10 mm) printed with the **same code** as the outer layer. This disc is hidden underneath.

When you are assigned to a base or ship, you receive a new main patch. The inner disc is *not* yet part of your stack – it stays hidden.

---

## 2. Initial State (Trainee on Earth)

- **Main patch:** `circle 01` (outer layer) with inner disc `01` underneath.
- **Stack:** empty (or optionally a single `01` disc if you consider your birthplace as already earned – but by standard, the first stack disc comes from leaving your first workplace).

No ritual needed because you haven’t changed bases yet.

---

## 3. Leaving a Base (e.g. Earth → Lunar Orbital Station `02’`)

When your tour at a base ends and you transfer to another location:

1. **The senior officer** conducts the “Base Gulp” ritual (see separate description).
2. **Peel off** the outer layer of your current main patch (e.g. `circle 01`). The Velcro releases with a distinctive sound.
3. **Underneath you find the inner disc** – a small flat disc with the code `01`.
4. **Extract that disc** and attach it to your **horizontal Snezhkov Stack** on your chest or sleeve.  
   - If your stack was empty, this becomes the first disc (place of birth or first workplace – by convention, it represents the place you are leaving).
   - If you already have a stack, add the new disc to the right (chronological order).
5. **If you performed EVAs** during your stay at this base, you also add a separate `E` disc immediately after the world disc.
6. **Put on your new main patch** for the destination (e.g. `circle 02’`). This new patch also has its own hidden inner disc (`02’`).

Result: your stack grows by one (or two) discs each time you leave a base. The discs are physically tied to the places you actually worked.

---

## 4. Adding an `E` Disc (Extravehicular Activity)

An `E` disc is **earned** when you perform at least one spacewalk (EVA) during your stay at a base or on a vessel.

- The EVA must be logged in the official logbook (digitally signed by the commander).
- After the mission is complete (or immediately after the EVA, depending on base rules), a separate `E` disc is added to your stack, **directly after** the world disc for that location.

Example:  
You worked on lunar orbital station `02’` and did three EVAs. Your stack after leaving that station: `01` `02’` `E` (then later `03` for Mars, etc.).

`E` discs are the same size as world discs and are placed touching their predecessor.

---

## 5. Returning to Earth

When you return to Earth after working elsewhere:

- Your main patch becomes `circle 01` again (new two‑layer patch with inner disc `01`).
- The previous main patch (e.g. `circle 03` for Mars) is peeled off; its inner disc (`03`) is added to your stack (plus any `E` discs earned there).
- Your stack now contains your birthplace (if you left Earth once) or a record of all your assignments.

---

## 6. Stack Layout Rules

- **Touching discs** – no gaps, no overlaps. The distance between centres equals the disc diameter.
- **Order** – left to right, chronological: first disc = earliest location (usually birthplace).  
- **Current location** – not duplicated in the stack; only the main patch shows it.
- **Maximum length** – no strict limit, but for physical patches 4–6 discs are practical. Longer stacks can be stored digitally.

---

## 7. What If You Lose a Disc or Patch?

- **RFID‑enabled patches** can be deactivated remotely and reissued.
- **Digital backup** of your stack is kept in the central registry (based on logbook entries).
- You can request a replacement physical patch from any base administrator.

---

## 8. Example: Full Donning Sequence for a Mars‑bound Astronaut

**Start on Earth:** main patch `01`, stack empty.

**After training on Earth orbit (6 months, no EVAs):**  
Leave Earth orbit → peel `01` outer layer, add inner disc `01` to stack. New main patch `01’`. Stack: `01` `01’`.

**After lunar orbital station (with EVAs):**  
Peel `01’`, add inner disc `01’` and an `E` disc. New main patch `02’`. Stack: `01` `01’` `E` `02’`.

**After Mars mission (no EVAs on Mars – surface walks are not EVA):**  
Peel `02’`, add inner disc `02’`. New main patch `03`. Stack: `01` `01’` `E` `02’` `03`.

Now the astronaut’s patch shows `03` (Mars), and the stack tells his full career.

---

## 9. Notes for Manufacturers

- Disc diameter: 8–10 mm.  
- Material: fabric with adhesive back or Velcro loop for stacking.  
- Two‑layer patch: outer layer with Velcro hook on back, inner layer with Velcro loop on front (to stick to outer) and adhesive/loop on back (to attach to stack).  
- Printing: high‑contrast monochrome (black on white or white on dark background).
