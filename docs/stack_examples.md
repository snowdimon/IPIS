# Stack Examples from Culture

The Snezhkov Stack works for any space traveller – real or fictional. Below are a few illustrative examples. Stack elements are shown as touching discs (the physical patch), and the main patch code is given separately.

---

## Major Tom (David Bowie, “Space Oddity”)

**Main patch** (if still in space): `circle 00` (deep space)  
**Stack:** `01` `01'` `E`  
- `01` – born on Earth  
- `01'` – trained on Earth orbit (long‑term mission)  
- `E` – performed at least one EVA (repair / spacewalk)  

*Note: Major Tom lost communication. In IPIS terms his stack would remain unchanged until a base logs his return.*

---

## John Carter (Edgar Rice Burroughs, Barsoom)

**Main patch** (when on Mars): `circle 03`  
**Stack:** `01` `03`  
- `01` – born on Earth  
- `03` – transported to Mars (long‑term residence)  

`E` is optional – if you count his flying and fighting as “extra‑vehicular” (Mars has an atmosphere, so strictly not EVA; better omit).

If he returns to Earth: main patch becomes `circle 01`, stack grows to `01` `03` `01` (showing two separate periods on Earth).

---

## Mark Watney (Andy Weir, “The Martian”)

**Main patch** (during mission): `circle 03`  
**Stack:** `01` `01'` `E`  
- `01` – born on Earth  
- `01'` – training / travel in Earth orbit  
- `E` – multiple EVAs on Mars (surface walks count as EVA because Mars has no breathable atmosphere)  

After rescue: main patch back to `01`, stack adds `03` (and possibly an `E` if the system logs surface EVAs as separate discs).

---

## Ellen Ripley (Alien franchise)

**Main patch** (on the Nostromo): `circle 00` (deep space)  
**Stack:** `01` `01'`  
- `01` – born on Earth  
- `01'` – orbital training / service  

After events on LV‑426, her stack would add `04` (assuming LV‑426 is designated `04` as a new world). This shows how IPIS can expand with new world numbers.

## Creating Your Own Fictional Stack

1. Decide birthplace (must be a surface world, e.g. `01` for Earth, `02` for Moon).  
2. Add work locations in order, each as a disc.  
3. Insert an `E` disc after any world where the character performed EVAs.  
4. The main patch shows the current location (or `00` if in deep space transit).  

You can use the [design kit](../assets/) to make physical patches for cosplay or fan art.
