🔥 The problem

🌍🚀

Growing chaos

Space is getting crowded – dozens of nations, private companies, mixed crews. No single simple language to instantly recognize who or what is in front of you.

❓

No visible experience

Flags and call signs don't show history. But in space, experience is critical for safety, trust, and efficient teamwork.

“Today in space it’s a mess of flags, mission logos, and corporate patches. IPIS is the first attempt to create an operating system for space identity.”

💡 IPIS – The solution

IPIS is a socio‑technical operating system. Rockets are hardware – IPIS is the software that enables different people, nations, and machines to work together without conflict.

🔵⬡

Simple coding

Circle (human/base) or hexagon (robot) + planet number (01 Earth, 02 Moon, 03 Mars) + index (' for orbit, E for EVA, 00 for deep space).

🏅

Snezhkov Stack

Horizontal row of touching discs – your accumulated space biography: birthplace, work locations, and EVAs. Wear your experience on your sleeve.

🛸

Ship Thread

Straight line on the hull from start to end node (e.g., 01—03). Intermediate manoeuvres are digital only. Clean, visible, unambiguous.

🔄

Two‑layer patch

No need to print new badges in space. Peel off the outer layer, the inner disc becomes your next stack element. Elegant and practical.

⚙️ Technical integration

📡

RFID patch

Passive chip for access control, automatic logging, and presence tracking. Works with existing base infrastructure.

🛰️

NORAD extension

Optional IPIS_CODE field in Space‑Track.org catalog – human‑readable identification for every satellite and spacecraft.

📋

Verification via logbooks

Stack entries are backed by digitally signed ship and base logbooks. No blockchain complexity – just reliable cryptography.

🍵 Ritual “Base Gulp”

1. A glass of local water.
2. A sip – symbolic acceptance into the ecosystem.
3. Handover of the new patch (sound of Velcro).

Voluntary, humane, bonding. It creates a sense of belonging and respect.

🤝

Why it matters

Isolated crews need more than technical standards – they need culture. “Base Gulp” makes IPIS alive.
